## Design Context

### Users
Friendy is for people who meet many people in real life and later remember the human context better than the name. The first concrete audience is event-heavy users such as Photon residency participants, founders, students, builders, and community people who add contacts after dinners, meetups, demos, or casual introductions. They are not looking for a CRM. They want a private, lightweight way to recover "the person who played piano at dinner" or "the recruiting agents founder" without manually organizing everyone.

### Brand Personality
Friendy should feel warm, private, and cute-cozy. The interface should feel like a trusted personal memory companion, not surveillance, not a sales tool, and not an enterprise contact database. The emotional target is calm relief: Friendy quietly remembers the thread of a meeting after people pass through each other's lives.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light-first, cozy-white visual world with warm paper surfaces, soft sky tints, small friendly character illustrations, and a living animated background. The hero should feel atmospheric like a gentle animated scene behind the website: little human characters meet, exchange a memory, and continue on. Pull useful qualities from the provided references: Superhuman's full-bleed atmospheric hero with floating product panels, Genie's friendly white-canvas illustration language, and General Intelligence Company's refined translucent layering. Avoid the darker Mercury/Raycast mood, heavy command-center framing, purple AI SaaS gradients, generic dashboards, and three-column feature grids that do not tell the Friendy product story.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with the memory moment. The first viewport should show "I remember the context, not the name" and Friendy finding the person, not an abstract AI-agent pitch.
2. Consent must be visible. Event tracking, contact detection, and memory saving should always read as user-approved, source-backed, and private.
3. Make the product feel human before technical. Use warm copy, rounded soft forms, small character moments, and message-like surfaces instead of CRM tables or analytics cards.
4. Use motion as atmosphere, not distraction. The background animation may run gently behind the landing page, but it must respect reduced motion and never obscure the core copy or product preview.
5. Keep the interface cozy and precise. Warm white canvas, soft shadows, compact but readable typography, and one restrained accent should replace generic SaaS chrome.
